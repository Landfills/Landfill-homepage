Landfill for New York: Art and Resiliency Workshop

Date: June 2017
 Location: Red Hook Public Library
7 Wolcott St.

Red Hook, Brooklyn, similar to the Rockaways, Queens faces the challenge of reimagining its future through innovative resiliency tools and strategies often originated within the community. On June 17, 2017 Landfill project took part in Building Resiliency 2017: A Day Of Action organized by RETI center to explore how Red Hook can become more resilient through local actions. Art and Resilience works—Äop participants discussed the reasons why and the ways how artists can play a role in the resiliency efforts and community-based planning, as well as how to test and implement those in Landfill for New York.

The workshop participants brought up a variety of reason why collaboration between artists and planner within a community-based planning process could be beneficial.
Those are some of the things that art can offer do for community-based planning:
Capture the momentum;
Communicate the process with a different language and visualize;
Make the conversation less formal and less scary;
Emphasise personal rather than institutional;
Build trust between the communities and institutions;
Organize;
Increase participation by adding art shows and talks to community meetings;
Nourish the research process;
Provide different ways of thinking and take process out of common set of rules;
Those are the things that art planning can do for the artists:
Bring input from the real world and inform work with new themes;
Become an art process in itself;
Help create change;
Connect to other ways of thinking;
Provide direct exposure to human experience;
Expand audience to reach people from outside the art world;
Unlock new non-art specific funding;

The RETI Center (The Resilience, Education, Training and Innovation Center) is a non-profit organization acting as a local hub dedicated to partnering with organizations in the education, business, and workforce development sectors to enhance and deepen their engagement in resilience. RETI Center provides services to support and transform existing efforts and creates new programming to help grow and sustain an economic future for New York City and open pathways for youth and underserved communities.
